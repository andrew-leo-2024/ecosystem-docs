# Universal Risk Taxonomy

Risk-speak is a rabbit hole -- once you start pulling the thread, every type of organization has its own shadow-language of dangers, anxieties, and controls. The words change with scale and sector, but the essence of fear is universal: **losing money, losing trust, losing control, losing purpose**.

This page catalogs the complete landscape of risk across 85+ domains, organized as a fractal taxonomy that scales from a bakery's flour delivery risk to the heat death of the universe. Every entry maps back to five primal roots.

---

## The Five Primal Roots of Risk

All risk -- no matter how exotic -- traces back to these five fears:

| Root | Core Question |
|---|---|
| **Survival** | Will we exist tomorrow? |
| **Resources** | Do we have what we need? |
| **Trust** | Do people believe in us? |
| **Control** | Can we govern what happens? |
| **Meaning** | Are we still relevant? |

What changes is the dialect. The bakery says "cash flow crunch," the multinational says "liquidity risk." Same beast, different mask.

## Part I: Organizational Risk Domains

### 1. Micro Business / Startup (5-Person Bakery)

Risk-speak sounds domestic, but it is existential:

- **Cash flow crunch** -- today's sales pay tomorrow's flour
- **Supplier dependency** -- one farmer, one broken van, no bread
- **Health & safety liability** -- a customer slips, or food poisoning
- **Regulatory surprise** -- inspection fines, sudden permits
- **Talent drain** -- losing the one baker who knows the secret recipe
- **Digital exposure** -- POS hacked, or bad Google review nukes reputation

---

### 2. Scaling SME / Mid-Market Company

- **Operational risk** -- process breaks, bottlenecks, untrained staff
- **Cyber risk** -- customer data leaks, ransomware
- **Compliance risk** -- tax laws, labor rules, GDPR-style regulations
- **Reputation risk** -- negative press, viral social media backlash
- **Concentration risk** -- too much revenue from one client, product, or geography
- **Succession risk** -- founder-dependent culture, weak middle management

---

### 3. Large Corporations & Multinationals

The language becomes MBA-gothic:

- **Strategic risk** -- bad M&A deals, disrupted business models
- **Regulatory and political risk** -- sanctions, tariffs, lobbying failures
- **Liquidity and credit risk** -- debt markets seizing, counterparty defaults
- **Operational resilience** -- pandemics, supply chain fragility, geopolitical shocks
- **Technology obsolescence risk** -- legacy IT dragging growth
- **ESG risk** -- climate exposure, activist investors, reputational activism
- **Talent & culture risk** -- toxic culture, strikes, leadership scandal

---

### 4. Public Sector & Government Bodies

Risk-speak here gets bureaucratic, often cloaked in euphemism:

- **Policy failure risk** -- programs that do not deliver intended outcomes
- **Public trust risk** -- corruption, transparency issues, citizen protests
- **National security risk** -- cyberattacks, terrorism, espionage
- **Service delivery risk** -- passport delays, failed welfare payments
- **Fiscal risk** -- budget overruns, pension liabilities, debt crises
- **Inter-agency coordination risk** -- silos leading to policy contradictions
- **Reputational sovereignty risk** -- international image, diplomatic fallout

---

### 5. Nonprofits & NGOs

Risk-speak soaked in mission and donor anxiety:

- **Funding risk** -- donor withdrawal, grant dependency
- **Mission drift risk** -- losing focus in pursuit of funding opportunities
- **Impact risk** -- programs not producing measurable change
- **Volunteer burnout risk** -- over-reliance on unpaid labor
- **Fraud and misuse risk** -- donor funds siphoned, damaging trust
- **Safety risk** -- staff and volunteers in conflict zones
- **Reputation risk** -- one scandal and donations evaporate

| Universal Archetype | Bakery Expression | Corporate Expression | Government Expression | Cosmic Expression |

| **Survival** | Cash flow crunch | Liquidity risk | Fiscal crisis | Existential risk |
